Long Island Iced Tea

Description

Long Island iced tea recipes are fun, fizzy and not for the faint of heart. The drink contains nearly five ounces of liquor, making it more alcoholic than your average martini. And yet, when made correctly, a Long Island cocktail tastes bright and bubbly, not boozy. Food Network Kitchen cracked the code to create this tasty recipe for a Long Island iced tea that you can customize with different spirits.

Ingredients

Yield: 2 drinks

  • 2 cups ice cubes
  • 1 ounce vodka
  • 1 ounce gin
  • 1 ounce white rum
  • 1 ounce white tequila
  • 1/2 ounce Triple Sec
  • 2 tbsp freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup cola
  • 2 lemon wedges

Steps

  1. Fill a cocktail shaker with ice. Pour the vodka, gin, rum, tequila, Triple Sec, and lemon juice into the shaker.
  2. Cover and shake vigorously to combine and chill.
  3. Pour the mixture, ice and all, into 2 glasses or beer mugs and top off with the cola.
  4. Garnish with lemon wedges. Serve.
